Mechanical Engineer / Computer Scientist

Liza Gaylord

My name is Liza Gaylord. I am a recent graduate from MIT with a double major in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (a joint major) and Mechanical Engineering. I started coding at the age of elevn and secured an internship working in software during high school. I turned a passion for computer science, a curiosity about everything, and a set of diverse interests into an acceptance to MIT.

I decided to pursue my double major because whenever I worked solely in builidng the hardware or coding the software, my understanding of a project felt incomplete. I wanted to understand the 'why' of each specification I was handed and the larger reason behind any problem I encountered. Why did the speed of the servo motor change despite no code changes? Because it's getting less power from our limited battery when other mechanical functions are running! A simple example, sure, but the more I knew about each part of the system, the easier it was the understand when things went wrong. This philosophy has led me to a broad technical background and an interest in systems as a whole.

I have never been content doing my piece and going home for the day. With every group project I have faced, I took on the responsibility of coordinating to ensure my team delivered the best possible product. I wrote specifications, made product roadmaps, created deadlines and sub-teams, set-up and ran user tests... whatever it took. I usually described this role as team leader and it was sometimes an official designation. However, a team leader in a group project in school wields no actual authority. Instead, I had to reply on my communication and interpersonal skills to ensure my teammates share the same understanding of our goals and restraints, regardless of their technical background.

This role, in truth, is product manager; it is what I have always done, and what I would like to continue doing. I am currently seeking a job in product management because I want to make my natural role my full-time career.
